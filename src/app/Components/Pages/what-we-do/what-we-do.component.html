We promote circular and regenerative innovations, including regenerative agriculture, natural buildings, waste management and sustainable energy techniques to support this mission.

Water management
Systems

Community-level water management programs

At Feedback to the Future, we are committed to reversing the downward water cycle caused by land degradation in Kenya. Many rivers have become seasonal due to unsustainable agricultural practices and deforestation, leading to water shortages and damage to aquatic ecosystems. We work closely with local communities to implement landscape water management strategies and promote regenerative agriculture.

By restoring degraded soils and allowing rainwater to penetrate the ground, we aim to revive the water cycle that has disappeared over time. Our efforts focus on restoring water availability, improving ecosystem health, and ensuring sustainable water resources for present and future generations.

Renewable energy

At FttF, we integrate circular and regenerative economy principles with renewable energy initiatives. Our focus is on exploring the transformative potential of renewable energy in local communities. Through living labs, we experiment with sustainable energy technologies to accelerate adoption. We facilitate knowledge sharing and partnerships between government, private sectors, and communities.

Our goal is resource efficiency and waste reduction through circular economy principles. By prioritizing local resources and harnessing Kenya's solar potential, we promote sustainable energy generation and economic growth. We empower communities, foster regenerative energy systems, and promote collaboration.

Natural building

The built environment contributes significantly to environmental degradation, especially in East Africa. To address this, adopting natural building practices using locally available and renewable resources is crucial. Natural materials like adobe, rammed earth, bamboo, timber, and thatch have a lower carbon footprint and minimal environmental impact.

They also promote local skills, generate income, and preserve culture. Natural buildings provide excellent thermal insulation, reducing energy consumption and greenhouse gas emissions. At Feedback to the Future, we promote and educate about the benefits of natural building through training and guidance.

Waste management

East Africa, particularly Kenya, is undergoing social and economic changes, including population growth, urbanization, and shifting consumer behaviors. FttF is dedicated to addressing waste management challenges through innovative solutions. Our living lab focuses on developing circular waste management models, such as converting organic waste into compost for sustainable food production.

We explore producing biogas from household organic waste for cooking, reducing reliance on traditional fuels, and promoting a cleaner environment. Additionally, we implement biological wastewater treatment solutions to minimize environmental impact and conserve water resources. Through partnerships and research, we aim to create a sustainable waste management system, fostering responsible practices and economic opportunities. Join us in building a future where waste is valued and our actions benefit the environment and community.

Circular neighborhoods in
informal settlements

At Feedback to the Future, we are actively involved in the development of circular neighborhoods in the slums of Nairobi. These neighborhoods are designed to promote sustainable practices and address the unique challenges faced by slum communities. By adopting circular economy principles, we aim to create self-sustaining communities that minimize waste, maximize resource efficiency, and improve the overall quality of life.

Our approach involves engaging with residents, community leaders, and stakeholders to co-create solutions that are tailored to the specific needs and context of the slums. We focus on implementing initiatives such as waste management systems, renewable energy solutions, and sustainable water and sanitation practices. Through the development of circular neighborhoods, we aim to create inclusive and resilient communities that provide access to basic services, promote economic opportunities, and enhance the overall well-being of slum residents. By transforming the slums into sustainable and thriving neighborhoods, we strive to break the cycle of poverty and create a brighter future for all.

At Feedback to the Future, we recognize the value of indigenous knowledge and cultural practices in understanding and addressing local challenges. We actively promote the collection and preservation of indigenous knowledge because it provides valuable insights into different areas and contributes to the identity of local communities. We believe that this knowledge is not only essential for solving local problems but also for fostering sustainable development. By incorporating indigenous knowledge into our work, we aim to honor and respect the wisdom of local communities while finding innovative solutions that are rooted in their cultural heritage.
